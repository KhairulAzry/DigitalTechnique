Electronic Instrument System

Cabin instrument panel

  • All instruments essential to the operation of an aircraft displayed on special panels in view to the aircrew.
  • Instruments are grouped according to their function on each panel.
  • The number and position of which vary accordance with the number of instruments, size of the aircraft, cockpit layout.
  • The main instrument panel is positioned in front of the pilot’s normal line of vision and contains the primary flight instruments.
  • Other panels are overhead panel, at the side, on control pedestal located centrally between the pilots.

 

Instrument grouping

  • Most aircraft instruments are grouped according to their function.
  • All instruments should be grouped on the instrument panel symmetrically about the vertical plane of the pilot vision.

 

Basic Six

There are 6 flight instruments that are necessary for the pilot to create a picture of aircraft current flight condition and to carry any control movement. These are:

  • AIRSPEED INDICATION
  • ARTIFICIAL HORIZONTAL
  • ALTIMETER
  • VERTICAL SPEED INDICATOR
  • COURSE INDICATOR
  • TURN AND BANK INDICATOR

 

Basic T

With the introduction of newer type of aircraft, the basic 6 layout was reviewed and a grouping known as BASIC T was introduced.

Within this group of 6, there are 4 key flight instruments placed directly in front of the pilot in a grouping known as the FLIGHT T. The FLIGHT T made of:

  • AIRSPEED INDICATOR
  • ARTIFICIAL HORIZON
  • ALTIMETER
  • COURSE DIRECTION INDICATOR

 

Engine Instrument

The positions of the instruments in the engine indicating group are arranged so that those related to each engine are position based on their positions on the aircraft.

 

Electronic Instrument

  • Electronic display instruments are basically colour TV displays that are presented according to signals generated by the computer.
  • The computer are commonly referred to as a SYMBOL GENERATOR.
  • The display referred to as CRT DISPLAY or MULTI-FUNCTIONAL DISPLAY.

 

Electronic Instrument Panel Layout

 

Captain's Main Instrument Panel

  • PFD – PRIMARY FLIGHT DISPLAY
    • Display : Attitude, Flight Director, Altitude and Airspeed
    • Also known as EADI (Electronic Attitude Direction Indicator)
  • ND – NAVIGATION DISPLAY
    • Display : Navigational information and Weather Radar
    • Also known as EHSI (Electronic Horizontal Situation Indicator)

 

F/O’S MAIN INSTRUMENT PANEL

 

PILOT CENTER INSTRUMENT PANEL

  • The center instrument panel is used for EICAS/ECAM.
  • The engine parameter and aircraft status information are displayed for aircrew.
  • The main EICAS/ECAM displayed the primary engine parameter such as Engine Pressure Ratio (EPR), Engine Gas Temperature (EGT), N1, Thrust mode and Total Air temperature (TAT).
  • Crew alerting warning, caution, advisory and memo messages are also presented on the main display.
  • Secondary engine parameter such as N2, oil pressure, oil quantity, oil temperature and engine vibration are displayed on auxiliary display.
