Numbering Systems

Decimal System

  • Comprise of 10 digits from 0 to 9
  • Base 10 system. Example 25410
  • POSITIONAL VALUE SYSTEM

Example : 25410 sonsists of 2 HUNDEREDS, 5 TENS and 4 ONE units.

Written as : (2 X 100) + (5 X 10) (4 X 1)
= (2 X 102) + (5 X 101) + (4 X 100)

Digit 2 carries the MOST weight and is known as MOST SIGNIFICANT DIGIT (MSD)

Digit 4 carries the LEAST weight and is known as LEAST SIGNIFICANT DIGIT (LSD)

 

Disadvantage in Digital Computer

10 Discrete Value Level which is extremely difficult to operate due to:

  • Any VARIATION of POWER SUPPLY would cause error
  • Component TOLERANCE MUST be ZERO
  • Component VALUE will change with AGE

 

BINARY SYSTEM

  • Comprise of 2 digits (0 & 1) known as BITS
  • POSITIONAL value system (Base 2 system. Example : 10112)
    10112=(1X23)+(0X22)+(1X21)+(1X20)
  • BINARY to DECIMAL Conversion
    10112=(1X23)+(0X22)+(1X21)+(1X20)
    = 8+0+2+1
    = 11
  • DECIMAL to BINARY Conversion. SUCCESSIVE POWER OF 2.
    27 = 16+8+2+1
    =24 + 23 + 21 + 20
    =(1X24)+(1X23)+(0X22)+(1X21)+(1X20)
    = 110112
  • Suitable for SMALL number
  • Any number converted into BINARY form, the binary numbers is known as a WORD.
  • Each word is formed of a numbers of BITS (BINARY DIGITS) and this represents the WORD LENGTH.
  • Example: 34710 = 1010110112
    So 1010110112 is a WORD. Word length is 9 because there is 9 bits.

 

OCTAL SYSTEM

  • Base 8 systems. Composed of 8 digits from 0 to 7.
  • OCTAL to DECIMAL conversion
    2758=(2x82)+(7x81)+(5x80)
    =128+56+5 = 18910
  • DECIMAL to OCTAL conversion
    Divide by 8 and Record any REMAINDER of division
  • OCTAL to BINARY conversion
    Convert eachOCTAL number to into 3 bits BINARY equivalent.
    6358 TO BINARY
    6    3    5
    110    011    101
    Thus, 6358= 1100111012
  • BINARY to OCTAL conversion. (1001110112 TO OCTAL)
    Divide BINARY number into groups of 3 BITS starting from LSB.
    100    111    011
    4    7    3
    Thus, 1001110112=4738
    If the FINAL group of MSB does NOT have 3 BITS, ADD enough ZERO to make up 3 BITS.

 

HEXADECIMAL SYSTEM

  • DECIMAL to HEX Conversion.
    Divide with 16 and take the REMAINDER of division.
  • HEX to BINARY Conversion. Convert HEX digit into 4 bits BINARY equivalent.
    B2F16 TO BINARY
    B    2    F
    1101    0010    1111
    THUS, B2F16= 1011001011112
  • BINARY to HEX conversion. Divide BINARY number into groups of 4 bits STARTING at LSB.
    1101101010012 TO HEX
    1101    1010    1001
    13    10    9
    D    A    9
    Thus, 1101101010012=DA916
  • HEX to OCTAL conversion and vice versa.
    • Convert HEX to BINARY
    • Convert BINARY to OCTAL
    OCTAL to HEX, just REVERSE the process.
    3D16 convert to OCTAL.
    • Convert HEX to BINARY,
      3    D
      0011    1101
      3D16 = 1111012
    • Convert BINARY to OCTAL
      111    101
      7    5
      1111012 = 758
    Thus, 3D16 = 758

 

BCD

  • Binary Coded Decimal
  • Number represented in 4 bits binary code
  • Leaving a space between each group of 4 digits
  • Example:
    • 1110 to BCD is 0001 0001
    • 1000 0101 in BCD to Decimal is 8510
